The native insects that eat the manure become free protein for the chickens. Combined with daytime pasture access, this slashes the most expensive line item in any poultry operation — feed.

### Technical Specifications & Engineering Details

**For farmers, builders, agricultural engineers, and compliance officers.**

#### Overall Dimensions & Capacity
- External footprint: **6050 mm length × 2430 mm width** (standard recycled 20 ft shipping container base)  
- Designed capacity: **100 laying hens**  
- Internal zones: 	Room 1: Main living area + dedicated nesting area + vermiculture pit below slatted floor
			Room 2: Maintenance/supply room 

#### Structural Framework & Materials
- Primary structure: Recycled 20 ft shipping container with reinforced openings and modifications  
- Internal wood framing: 50 × 100 mm and 100 × 100 mm Pine Wood 
- Additional structural support: 30 × 60 mm steel box frame 
- Walls & ceilings: PUR board insulation with moisture protection and plastic panel finish (where specified)  
- Vermiculture pit: Food-grade epoxy coating for corrosion protection and hygiene  
- Aluminum components: Manure deflector + chick protection panels  
- Natural daylight openings: Polycarbonate panels (4 total)  
- Ventilation openings: Louvered design to reduce wind speed while maintaining convection airflow (hot air extraction at high level, fresh air intake at low level)

#### Flooring & Vermiculture System
- Removable slatted flooring over the full dedicated vermiculture zone  
- Chicken manure falls through slats into the pit  
- Native flies and worms consume nitrogen compounds and manure in approximately **6 days** (95%+ reduction vs traditional composting)  
- Mature worms spill over into chicken areas as natural protein source (~20% feed replacement)  
- Pit protected by food-grade epoxy coating; designed for minimal maintenance and zero odor

#### Nesting & Egg Collection System
- 18 nesting compartments (compartment width optimized to 300 mm for dedicated nesting function)  
- Roll-away egg collection design  
- Eggs roll to centralized collection tray located inside a panel box in the maintenance/supply room  
- Allows egg harvesting without entering the main chicken living area  
- Minimizes egg contact with manure and maximizes cleanliness

#### Feeding System
- Gravity-fed closed feed hopper (labor-saving, constant access)  
- Feeding line / trough running the length of the main living area  
- Designed for minimal waste and easy refilling from the maintenance room

#### Watering System
- 55-gallon (≈208 L) drum reservoir mounted on stand  
- Water supply pipe feeding nipple drinker line  
- Gravity or low-pressure delivery for reliable hydration with minimal moving parts

#### Ventilation & Airflow
- Multiple high-level and low-level ventilation openings with louvers  
- Engineered for natural convection: hot air exits high, cooler fresh air enters low  
- Louver design reduces wind speed and drafts while maintaining effective air exchange  
- Supports healthy flock environment and maximum egg production
- *Pre-Order customers will receive infrared reflective roof coating on their Chicken Fortress at no additional cost

#### Lighting & Animal Welfare Features
- 4 polycarbonate natural daylight openings for abundant natural light (supports higher egg output and pasture-raised egg quality)  
- Automatic electric chicken door with timed control and sloped entrance ramp  
- Large maintenance door for human access  
- Overhead perches sized for 100 birds  
- Daytime pasture access via automatic door; predator protection at night  
- Aluminum protection panels for chicks where required

#### Maintenance & Operational Notes
- Self-cleaning vermiculture pit requires minimal intervention  
- Slatted floor sections are removable for occasional deep inspection or cleaning if needed  
- Egg collection, feeder refilling, and water checks performed from the maintenance room  
- Designed for low labor input and high reliability in tropical and temperate climates
